# Ian MacCallum — Personal Brand & Portfolio Marketing Plan

> Personal portfolio + brand hub for Ian MacCallum. Software engineer, founder, shipper-of-things.
> Domain: `ian.maccallum.dev` | Stack: Next.js 16, Tailwind, Framer Motion, Geist.

---

## TL;DR

This is not a SaaS marketing plan. There's nothing to "convert." The portfolio is **top-of-funnel for Ian's career and Ian's projects** — the hub the spokes point back to.

Every project ships → portfolio gets updated → social drives traffic → portfolio funnels people to the next step (download an app, hire him, intro him, follow him, subscribe).

The 13 apps and sites listed on `ian.maccallum.dev` (Thoughtful, Encore, Claude Drops, Tax Days, Ideas, Girl Math, Only Recipes, No Context Bot, Mojo, DIYProject.ai, Cat IQ, Dog IQ, Parra) **are the marketing.** Each one earns surface area on the App Store, on X, on Google. The portfolio is where that surface area compounds into a personal brand.

**The big idea:** Ian's portfolio should function like @levelsio's `levels.io` — public scoreboard, build log, reputation surface — but tuned for **opportunity flow** (clients, advisors, press, intros) rather than direct product revenue.

**Why this matters:** Ian already ships at a rate few people match (13 visible products, ex-Universe YC W18 Staff Eng, Principal at Stable Kernel, founder of Parra). The shipping is done. **The distribution is the gap.** Traffic comes from the App Store and word of mouth. No project links back to `ian.maccallum.dev` consistently. No newsletter. X is active but not optimized for compounding follows. His name doesn't dominate page 1 of Google.

**#1 action this week:** Set up a Beehiiv newsletter ("Ship Log"). Add a single email-capture block above the projects grid: *"Get the monthly shipping log — what I built, what I learned, what flopped."* Write issue 1 this Sunday covering everything shipped in the last 30 days. Every other channel feeds it. It survives X going down, Apple changing the App Store, or Google changing search.

## Competitor / Reference Snapshot

These are **not competitors** — there are no competitors for "being Ian." These are **reference brands** to study and steal from.

**@levelsio (`levels.io`)** — Public revenue dashboard, 600K+ X followers, ~50% of traffic from Twitter. Photo AI ~$132K/mo. **Steal:** "public scoreboard" page (every project, MRR, status, App Store rating). **Skip:** contrarian-bro tone (Stable Kernel clients read this).

**@marclou (`marclou.com` / ShipFast)** — "Just Ship It" newsletter hit 20K subs. **Steal:** newsletter-first funnel (every tweet ends with newsletter CTA), short screenshot-heavy tweets. **Skip:** boilerplate-as-business-model — Ian's leverage is apps, not info products.

**@theo (`theo.gg`)** — YouTube + Twitter, T3 Stack creator, bridge between indie maker and "real eng" audiences. **Steal:** strong stack opinions ("here's why I use SwiftData + CloudKit"). **Skip:** YouTube as primary channel — too much production for current bandwidth.

**swyx (`swyx.io`)** — "Learn in Public" essay went viral; "Rise of the AI Engineer" (2023, endorsed by Karpathy) defined a category. **Steal:** ONE signature long-form essay that becomes the calling card. Candidates: *"13 apps in 18 months,"* *"Why I'm betting on native iOS in the LLM era,"* *"The senior engineer's guide to indie hacking."* Also: newsletter as home base — Twitter is rented land.

**Dan Abramov (`overreacted.io`)** — Personal blog, deeply thoughtful, ranks #1 for his name. **Steal:** aesthetic restraint. The portfolio already has this; a `/writing` section in Geist would extend it.

**Default takeaways from research:** Newsletter > Twitter for compounding. Visual content = 3x engagement on X. Short tweets (<200 chars) outperform. Personalization beats template for podcast pitches (hosts get 100+/mo, 99% generic). Schema + Person markup + consistent handles = own page 1 for your name.

### TWITTER / X — 5 Post Formats

#### Format 1 — Project Launch (use for every new app or major release)

```
Just shipped: [App Name]

[One-line description of what it does and who it's for]

[Specific feature or unique angle in 1 line]

Built with [stack — Swift 6, SwiftData, Claude, etc.]

[App Store link or URL]

[Screenshot or 5-second video]
```

**Example for Encore:**
```
Just shipped: Encore.

A printable QR code for live musicians.
Tips, song requests, feedback — all in one link.

Built with Next.js + Stripe + a lot of late nights at venues.

encorelivemusic.app

[gif of the QR card]
```

**Why it works:** Short, visual, ends with a link, includes stack signal for the dev audience.

---

#### Format 2 — Lesson Learned (1-2 per week)

```
[Counter-intuitive observation in 1 sentence.]

[Context — what you did.]

[What you learned — the actual lesson.]

[Optional: what you'll do next time.]
```

**Example:**
```
Made way more money charging $19/year than $1/month.

Same app. A/B tested it across two apps last quarter.

Annual feels like "trying it" — monthly feels like a subscription. People hate subscriptions.

Going annual-only on the next two launches.
```

**Why it works:** Specific, contrarian-adjacent, has a number, has a forward-looking commitment.

---

#### Format 3 — Contrarian Take (1-2 per month — use sparingly)

```
Unpopular opinion: [the take]

[1-2 sentences of why]

[1 sentence of nuance / when it doesn't apply]
```

**Example:**
```
Unpopular opinion: SwiftData is fine, actually.

I've shipped 8 apps on it in the last year. Zero migrations gone wrong. The Apple devrel discourse is louder than the actual pain.

(Caveat: if you need real querying, you'll hit walls. For most consumer apps you won't.)
```

**Why it works:** Stakes a claim, backs it with reps (8 apps), shows the nuance so it doesn't read as bait.

---

#### Format 4 — Screenshot Tease (2-3 per week)

```
[Single sentence — what we're looking at]
[Screenshot]
```

**Example:** *"Tax Days now supports the UK Statutory Residence Test. 17 jurisdictions tracked."* + clean SRT screenshot.

**Why it works:** Visual = 3x engagement. Low effort. Builds in-progress narrative without forcing a lesson.

---

#### Format 5 — Milestone / Numbers (1-2 per month — be honest)

```
[Milestone in 1 line]
[1-2 lines of context — when started, what changed]
[1 line of gratitude or forward direction]
```

**Example:**
```
Thoughtful crossed 10K downloads.
Shipped Oct 2025. Took 7 months and 14 paywall iterations to get here.
Thanks to everyone who sent a card. The next version is going to be wild.
```

**Why it works:** Specific number, real horizon, real iteration count (credibility), ends with anticipation.
